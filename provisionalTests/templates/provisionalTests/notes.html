Introduction

The Law in Zimbabwe requires that, before advancing to the steering wheel one should be a holder of a Provisional Learner's licence. The Provisional Learner's consists of 25 multiple choice questions and you are expected to score at least 22 points.

The new structure of questions requires that you possess a sound knowledge of practical driving techniques and make use of common driving sense. Best way is to read such questions fast and use only logical driving reasoning when choosing the correct answer.

Please note that it is usually only the theoretical aspect of driving that you may pass through using this tutorial. The road test requires much more practical teaching techniques that are beyond this tutorial's scope.

ABOUT MULTIPLE CHOICE TEST

Requirements

1)Three (black & white) D.L photos.

2)Valid Passport or National Identity Card (ID).

3)A Registration/Booking fee

Pass Marks

Classes 2, 3, 4&5 = 22/25 = 88%

Class One = 100%

Test's Duration

You have to answer the 25 questions in about 8 minutes

INTERSECTIONS & DIAGRAM RULES

We use the following rules only at intersections not controlled by the Police and when answering diagram questions.

RULE 1

No car should turn right in the face of oncoming traffic.

RULE 2

A car should stop and giveway to any traffic approaching from its right.

RULE 3

Where there is more than two cars: A car can move to the centre if it has nothing approaching from its right.

RULE 4

Where there is more than two cars and neither of them is turning right: Traffic in the same road can move at the same time.

RULE 5

At a roundabout give the right of way to traffic that is already circulating and all that is approaching from your right.

RULE 6

At CONTROLLED INTERSECTIONS any car facing a Stop or Giveway Sign is the last car to go.

EFFECTS AND PURPOSE OF CONTROLS

Accelerator

Pedal is situated on the right side.

Purpose being to increase speed.

Is operated by the right foot.

Brake

Pedal situated on the centre.

Purpose being to slow down the vehicle.

It is operated by the right foot.

Clutch

Pedal situated on the left side.

Purpose being to engage/disengage gears.

It is operated independently by the left foot.

Gear Lever

The lever is situated at the centre.

Purpose being to select gears.

Operated by the left or right hand depending on its position

HAND SIGNALS

Raising your hand up and down slowly is signal for slowing down.

Pointing your hand to the right signals turning right.

Rolling your hand forward and backward in circles signals turning left.

Raising your hand up signals stopping.

MANOUVERING PROCEDURES

Starting vehicle

1.Check the handbrake.

2.Depress the clutch.

3.Put gear into neutral.

4.Turn key on and check dashboard indications.

5.Start engine

Changing of gears, up or down

1.Release accelerator.

2.Depress the clutch.

3.Engage into required gear.

4.Release the clutch.

5.Accelerate or decelerate depending on desired course of action.

Turning Right Procedure

1.Check mirror.

2.Show desired intention.

3.Brake.

Slow down.

Select suitable gear and then turn.

Lane changing Procedure

1.Check rear view mirror.

2.Put on electrical signal.

3.Check blind spot.

4.Make hand signal.

Switch off Procedure

1.Release accelerator.

2.Depress clutch.

3.Put gear into neutral.

4.Engage handbrake.

Turn off engine immediately if non turbo charged.

Pull of to stop procedure

1.Check the mirror.

2.Reduce speed.

3.Check blind spot.

4. Pull off and stop.

Going straight Procedure at uncontrolled intersection

1.Slow down.

2.Check both sides and Give way to traffic from your right

3.If clear, Continue on straight as desired.

STOPPING DISTANCES

Drivers should always leave a safe following Distances When travelling behind a vehicle with no intention of overtaking, always divide your car speed by the number 15. The result you get is the number of cars you leave a gap for in front of your vehicle.
